Research

Connecting mechanisms across scales

My work combines atomistic simulation, free-energy methods, and data-driven modeling to understand and predict materials behavior.

01

Deformation in titanium alloys

Identifying competing deformation modes and structure transformations in metastable β-Ti alloys.

02

Phase transformations

Mapping temperature-dependent free-energy landscapes with metadynamics to reveal β-to-α″ transformation pathways.

03

Alloy corrosion

Studying cathodic activity, oxide defects, and localized corrosion at interfaces in aluminum and chromium systems.

04

Scientific machine learning

Developing efficient models for composition- and temperature-dependent misfit strain energy along phase boundaries.
